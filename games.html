How SJohnson Builds

Every game starts as a vibe-first sketch—feel over feature lists. I iterate fast, throw out what doesn't click, and keep the stuff that makes you want to hit "restart" just one more time. The goal is always that spark of joy you got from arcade cabinets and shareware demos.

Rapid Prototyping

I build quick playable slices to test ideas before committing. If it's not fun in 20 minutes, it won't be fun in 20 hours—so I pivot fast and keep experimenting.

Feel-First Controls

Movement and input response come before everything else. Tight, snappy controls make even simple mechanics feel satisfying—like the difference between a good jump and a great jump.

Crunchy Retro UI

Menus with chunky buttons, bold colors, and that satisfying click. UI should feel like part of the game world, not an afterthought—think arcade cabinets and DOS prompts.

Pixel-Art Accents

Strategic use of pixel art for sprites, icons, and UI elements. Not everything needs to be retro, but those nostalgic touches add character and charm that resonates with indie game fans.

Playful Audio Direction

Sound design and music collaboration to nail that arcade energy. Blips, boops, and catchy loops that get stuck in your head—audio is half the vibe.
